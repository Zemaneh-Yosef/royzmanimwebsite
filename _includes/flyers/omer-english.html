Halachic time-rulings

Source: Yalkut Yosef vol. 22 page 85 onwards - 5779 edition | Hazon Ovadia Yom Tov, page 232 onwards
  1. It is preferable to count each of the Omer days at Nightfall (which happens at 13.5 minutes after sunset, lengthened through latitude and season/length-of-the-day), allowing the entire count to be holistic (completed nights).
    Nevertheless, although intentional delays (such as stringent Nightfall calculations) are discouraged, one who missed that day should still count whenever possible (as long as it's still within that day - see #3).
  2. Recounting during the day is customary in case the night count was missed. Nevertheless, the blessing for that day is not recited after Dawn (even in an uncertain Dawn).
  3. The interval between sunset and Nightfall may be used leniently for either the prior (for those who forgot) or subsequent day, provided it is not simultaneously assigned to both.
    Therefore, a minyan praying ערבית after sunset should count the omer even before Nightfall, as the congregants may neglect to count otherwise.
  4. If one entirely misses a day’s count (from its Nightfall until the next day's Nightfall), they should appoint someone to recite future blessings on their behalf.
