Calculation Explanation

Halachic Dawn (which is when fasts start; S"A Orach Chaim 562) is calculated as 72 minutes before sunrise (Halikhot Olam Vol. I pg. 106). Locations further north or south of Israel will change this number relative to how far one is from the equator ("standard deviation"), transforming the minutes to 80 minutes in NY (Halacha Berurah, intro. to Chapter 261, Halacha 13). This time is then further shortened/elongated based on the length of the day ("seasonal minutes").

The calculation used for Halachic Nightfall spans 20 minutes after sunset, based on a stringency to accommodate the Rambam's opinion on Nightfall (Ḥazon Ovadia, Arba Ta'aniyot, pg. 12) - there is no need to accommodate any other stringency for any fast (with the exception of Yom Kippur), even in the United States. Nevertheless, the non-stringent time of 13.5 minutes is still the definition of nightfall, meaning one can break the fast as early as then. The changes made to Dawn ("standard deviation", "seasonal minutes") are also included here, measured relative to the short time of 13.5/20
