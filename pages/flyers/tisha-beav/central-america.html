Calculation Explanation

Our fast starts at Sunset of the previous day, which is defined with the complete setting of the sphere in the west (Halacha Berurah, intro. to Chapter 261 Halacha 21). One should refrain from eating a minute or two prior as precautionary steps (Ḥazon Ovadia, Arba Ta'aniyot, pg. 275-278; unlike Yom Kippur, this is not a commandment)

Fasts end at Halachic Nightfall, which we measure as 20 seasonal minutes after sunset (Ḥazon Ovadia, Arba Ta'aniyot, pg. 12), adjusted for latitudinal deviation (Halacha Berurah, intro. to Chapter 261, Halacha 13). Since this time is already a stringency (the letter of the law dictates one only needs to wait 13.5 [adjusted-]seasonal minutes, Yabia Omer Y"D 7:41), one need not layer any more stringency, for any of the fasts (with the exception of Yom Kippur), even in the USA.
