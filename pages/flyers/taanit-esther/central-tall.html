Calculation Explanation

Halachic Dawn signifies the commencement of a fast; individuals intending to rise before this time to eat should make a prior stipulation before sleeping. (Shulchan Aruch, Oraḥ Ḥayim 564)

This time is calculated as 72 minutes before sunrise (Halikhot Olam, Vol. I, p. 19), with adjustments based on their geographic location relative to Israel ("standard deviation"; Halacha Berurah, introduction to Chapter 261, Halacha 13) and the length of the day ("seasonal minutes"; Halikhot Olam, ibid).

Halachic Nightfall denotes the beginning of a new day in the Jewish calendar, transitioning the day away from the fast and into Purim. Purim features a separate prohibition of having a full meal until they have fulfilled their obligation to hear the Megillah reading. However, one is permitted to snack on bread or cake/cookies up to a kabetza (i.e., up to 54 grams), consume fruits and vegetables in quantities exceeding a kabetza (54 grams), and drink tea or coffee. Those who stringently refrain from tasting anything will receive special blessings.
This time is calculated at 13.5 minutes after sunset (Halikhot Olam, Vol. I, p. 250), with the same adjustments from Dawn applied. Individuals wishing to conclude the fast and listen to the Megillah later into the night (such as women) should instead wait 20 minutes (Ḥazon Ovadia, Arba Ta'aniyot, p. 12).
