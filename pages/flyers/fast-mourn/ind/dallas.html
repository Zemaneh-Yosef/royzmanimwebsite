Calculation Explanation

Halachic Dawn (which is when fasts start; S"A Orach Chaim 562) is measured as 72 seasonal minutes before sunrise (Halikhot Olam Vol. I pg. 106). Locations further north or south of Israel will need to factor standard deviation, turning the time pre-seasonal factoring to 80 minutes in NY (Halacha Berurah, intro. to Chapter 261, Halacha 13)

Halachic Nightfall is calculated (for our purposes) as 20 seasonal minutes after sunset. Although one can rely on the letter of the law that uses 13.5 minutes instead (Yabia Omer YD 7:41), the recommended (albeit not strictly necessary) practice is to be stringent and hold by 20 minutes to accommodate the Rambam's opinion on nightfall (Ḥazon Ovadia, Arba Ta'aniyot, pg. 12). One need not wait any longer than this time for any fast (with the exception of Yom Kippur), even in the USA.
